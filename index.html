Traditional Moroccan Dessert Library

Moroccan sweets are known for their rich use of honey, almonds, sesame, orange blossom water, dates, and warm spices. This collection highlights some of the most loved pastries and cookies found in Moroccan homes, bakeries, and Ramadan tables.

Honey & Sesame

Chebakia

Chebakia is one of Morocco’s most iconic sweets, especially popular during Ramadan. It is a flower-shaped fried cookie flavored with sesame, anise, cinnamon, and orange blossom water, then coated in honey for a glossy finish.

Almond Pastry

Briouat

Briouat is a crisp triangular pastry often filled with sweet almond paste. The pastry is fried until golden and finished with warm honey, creating a rich and festive dessert commonly served on special occasions.

Gazelle Horns

Kaab El Ghazal

Kaab El Ghazal, also called gazelle horns, is a refined Moroccan pastry made with a delicate outer dough and a fragrant almond filling scented with orange blossom water. Its crescent shape makes it one of the most elegant sweets in Moroccan baking.

Roasted Flour Sweet

Sellou (Sfouf)

Sellou is a rich Moroccan sweet made from toasted flour, ground almonds, sesame seeds, butter, and warm spices. It has a soft, crumbly texture and is especially associated with Ramadan and celebrations.

Classic Cookie

Ghriba Bahla

Ghriba Bahla is a rustic Moroccan cookie known for its cracked top and tender, melt-in-the-mouth texture. It is simple, traditional, and loved for its buttery flavor and home-style appearance.

Coconut Cookie

Ghriba Coconut

This variation of ghriba combines the familiar crumbly texture of Moroccan cookies with sweet shredded coconut. It is light, slightly chewy inside, and often dusted or decorated for a bakery-style finish.

Almond Cookie

Ghriba Almond

Ghriba Almond is a soft Moroccan cookie made with ground almonds and sugar, often producing a delicate interior with a slightly crisp surface. It is one of the most beloved tea-time sweets in Moroccan homes.

Date-Filled

Makrout

Makrout is a semolina-based pastry filled with sweet date paste and commonly fried or baked before being dipped in honey. Its combination of grainy semolina and soft date filling gives it a rich and distinctive texture.

Moroccan Biscotti

Fekkas

Fekkas is a twice-baked Moroccan biscuit similar to biscotti. It is usually filled with almonds, sesame seeds, raisins, or chocolate, and is especially popular with coffee or Moroccan mint tea.

Honey Pancake

Baghrir

Baghrir is often called the thousand-hole pancake because of its many tiny bubbles on the surface. It is soft, spongy, and commonly served warm with melted butter and honey.

Layered Flatbread

Msemen with Honey

Msemen is a flaky, layered Moroccan pancake or flatbread. When served sweet with honey or sugar, it becomes a comforting breakfast or dessert item with a chewy and buttery texture.

Semolina Cake

Harcha Sweet

Harcha is a griddled semolina bread with a slightly crumbly texture. Sweet versions are often served with honey, jam, or butter and are appreciated for their golden crust and comforting flavor.

Syrup Cake

Basbousa

Basbousa is a moist semolina cake soaked in sweet syrup. Though enjoyed across many regions, it is also appreciated in Moroccan dessert spreads for its tender texture and rich sweetness.

Royal Dessert

Jawhara

Jawhara is a luxurious Moroccan dessert made with crispy pastry layers, creamy milk filling, and almond garnish. It has a rich, elegant presentation and is often served at celebrations and formal gatherings.

Honey Spiral

Zlabia

Zlabia is a deep-fried spiral or twisted sweet soaked in syrup or honey. It has a glossy surface, a chewy interior, and a festive appearance that makes it stand out on dessert tables.

Moroccan Donut

Sfenj

Sfenj is a traditional Moroccan donut known for its airy interior and crisp outer layer. It is usually enjoyed plain, with sugar, or dipped in honey, especially as a warm street-food style treat.

Sweet Bread

Krachel

Krachel is a sweet Moroccan brioche flavored with anise, sesame, and orange blossom. Its soft bread-like texture makes it a favorite for breakfast, tea, or light dessert platters.

Festive Favorite

Almond Briouat with Honey

This sweet version of briouat features a rich almond filling wrapped in thin pastry, fried until crisp, and coated in honey. It is a classic holiday and Ramadan dessert with a glossy, bakery-style finish.

Modern Twist

Chocolate Fekkas

Chocolate Fekkas is a modern adaptation of the traditional Moroccan biscuit. It combines the crunch of classic fekkas with cocoa or chocolate pieces for a richer, more contemporary dessert option.

Date Pastry

Date Briouat

Date Briouat replaces almond filling with soft, naturally sweet dates. The result is a warm, caramel-like pastry that pairs especially well with Moroccan tea and festive dessert assortments.

Bakery Style

Pistachio Moroccan Cookies

Pistachio Moroccan cookies bring a colorful and slightly modern look to the dessert table. They usually feature a tender cookie base with crushed pistachios for extra flavor, color, and texture.

Floral Flavor

Orange Blossom Cookies

Orange blossom cookies highlight one of the signature aromas of Moroccan baking. They are delicate, fragrant, and often served at tea gatherings where subtle floral flavor is appreciated.

Festive Sweet

Honey Almond Balls

Honey almond balls are small celebratory sweets made with nuts and sweet glaze, often presented in decorative paper cups. They are popular for parties, Eid, and formal dessert trays.

Crunchy & Sweet

Sesame Honey Cookies

These cookies combine the nutty depth of sesame with the sweetness of honey. They are often crisp, golden, and especially appealing for people who enjoy traditional North African dessert flavors.

Coconut Filling

Coconut Briouat

Coconut Briouat offers a tropical variation on the classic pastry. The sweet coconut filling contrasts with the crisp outer shell, creating a dessert that feels both traditional and slightly modern.

Chocolate Pastry

Chocolate Briouat

Chocolate Briouat is a creative twist on the classic Moroccan pastry. It keeps the crisp pastry shell while introducing a smooth chocolate center, making it especially appealing in modern bakeries.

Date Filled

Stuffed Date Cookies

Stuffed date cookies are soft pastries filled with sweet date paste, often shaped decoratively and baked until lightly golden. They are a common feature in homemade Moroccan dessert assortments.

Soft Almond Sweet

Moroccan Almond Macaroons

Moroccan almond macaroons are chewy and rich, with a sweet almond base that gives them a delicate texture. They are often decorated simply but stand out for their flavor and softness.

Contemporary Favorite

Moroccan Chocolate Cookies

Moroccan chocolate cookies reflect the modern side of Moroccan home baking, combining traditional hospitality with contemporary ingredients. They are often served alongside more classic almond and sesame sweets.
